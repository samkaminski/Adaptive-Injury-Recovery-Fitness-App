1. Project Briefing 
Goals and Impact of a Successful Project 
We are building a mobile app that guides users safely through exercise-based rehabilitation 
after common injuries. Traditional fitness apps prioritize intensity and performance, which can 
be unsafe for injured users. Our system instead adapts workouts based on pain, soreness, and 
readiness signals to prioritize recovery and prevent re-injury. 
The MVP will focus on one injury type (e.g., knee or ankle) and implement a closed feedback 
loop: 
onboarding → personalized workout → user feedback → automatic adaptation → progress 
tracking. 
Success will be evaluated by: 
●  Users completing sessions consistently 
●  Reduced reported pain over time 
●  Safe, gradual progression through rehab stages 
●  Positive usability feedback in testing 
In the short term, the project demonstrates a working adaptive rehab system. In the long term, 
this approach could support additional injuries, clinician integration, and broader health use 
cases. 
Compared to generic workout apps, our solution is: 
●  safer 
●  recovery-focused rather than performance-focused 
●  more approachable for non-athletes 
The primary user group is individuals returning to exercise after injury, especially those without 
regular access to a physical therapist. This supports the course’s “not only you” requirement by 
designing for users with pain, mobility limits, and higher safety needs rather than healthy college 
students. 
 
Background Information 
Existing fitness apps emphasize strength or aesthetics, not recovery. Rehabilitation is typically 
managed through paper handouts or in-person physical therapy, with limited personalization 
between visits. 
We identified an opportunity to: 
●  digitize rehab plans 
●  collect daily recovery signals 
●  adapt exercises automatically 
●  provide clear progress feedback 
Key challenges include: 
●  encoding safe progression rules 
●  avoiding unsafe recommendations 
●  designing an interface that is calm and non-intimidating 
●  keeping scope realistic for one semester 
We mitigate risk by limiting scope to one injury type and a small exercise library.

3. Literature & Technology Review 
Technology Stack 
We selected: 
●  React Native for cross-platform mobile development 
●  Firebase for authentication and data storage 
●  Cloud Functions for adaptation logic  
These tools reduce backend overhead and allow faster iteration, which is critical for a semester 
project. 
 
Related Solutions 
Current fitness platforms (e.g., Strava, general workout apps) focus on performance metrics 
rather than injury recovery. Physical therapy apps often provide static programs without 
real-time personalization. 
Our approach differs by: 
●  daily readiness check-ins 
●  rule-based progression and regression 
●  recovery milestones instead of performance goals 
●  a calm, supportive UX  
 
Research Insights 
Rehabilitation best practices emphasize: 
●  gradual load increases 
●  pain-aware adjustments 
●  staged progression 
●  rest and recovery periods  
Our system encodes these principles through deterministic rules rather than opaque machine 
learning, ensuring safety and transparency. 
 
AI Usage 
AI will be used selectively to: 
●  assist development (code generation, testing) 
Core safety decisions will remain rule-based to maintain explainability and trust.
